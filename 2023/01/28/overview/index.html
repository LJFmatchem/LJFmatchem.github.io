Thermodynamics of reversible batteries

Basics of thermodynamics of electrochemistry

Faraday's Law

During electrolysis, the amount of substance precipitated on the electrode is proportional to the excess charge. \[ \begin{align} &Q=nzF\\ &Q_t=\sum_i n_iz_iF+Q_{DL} \end{align} \] Where the faraday constant is \[ F=\left(6.023\times10^{23} electrons/mol\right) \times\left(1.602\times10^{-19}C/electrons\right)=96487C/mol \approx96500C/mol \] The equaiton (2) represent the faraday law applied in a more sepecific condition of electrochemial process. The first term \(\sum_i n_iz_iF\) is sum of the excess charge of parallel electrochemical reactions. The second term \(Q_{DL}\) relate to the charging current.

The zeroth law of thermodynamics

Several phases/systems in contact with each other are in thermal equilibrium condition: all strength properties (temperature, pressure, chemical potential, electrochemical potential) of each phase/system are equal
