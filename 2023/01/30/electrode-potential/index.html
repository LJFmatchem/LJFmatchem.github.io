Electrode potential

Several basic properties related to electrode potential

Figure 1: The definition of several potential relate to the surface excess charges and chemical potential of conductor

Outer Potential/ Volta Potential

Definition: In vacuum, the electric work done by transferring the unit negative charge from infinity to point away from phase is called the external potential of conductor . The whole process only do electric work.

Because the point A is still in vacuum, The short-range force betweem the charged particle (ion, electron etc.) and conductor () has not yet occurred. The outer potential is measurable.

Surface Potential

Definition: The electric work done by transferring the unit negative charge from point away from the conductor phase , across the interface dipole layer, to point C in the phase is called the surface potential of conductor . The whole process only do electric work.

Apparently, in this transferring process, there are not only electric work but chemical effect work (chemical potential). However, It is impossible to separate electrical work or chemical effect work from the transferring process. Besides, is closely relate to the properties of conductor material and its surface structure. Therefore, is unmeasurable.

Figure 2. Causes of metal surface potential

In the bulk phase, the density of free electrons is equal everywhere. In the metal surface,the electrons are imbalanced and tend to come out of the surface and enter the vacuum to reduce the kinetic energy. The distance between these electrons and the positive ion nucleus increases, and the potential energy increases. The two factors work together to reach an equilibrium. The electron density in the surface area decreases with the distance from the surface.

Spill Over: When the temperature rises, some electrons are excited and quantum tunneling effect occurs, so they can be distributed within 0.1-0.2nm away from the metal surface
